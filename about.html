About Me

Always growing, always learning

Hey there!

I'm Jessica—I live in Washington, DC and lead Community Growth at the Centre for Effective Altruism. My team of ~15 people works across groups, courses, and digital products to help people find ways to do more good in the world.

How I got here

Back in 2016, I watched Peter Singer's TED talk and fell down the effective altruism rabbit hole. I got involved with the EA community during college at Yale, where I studied computer science and ended up running Yale EA.

After graduation, I kept coming back to this idea: the EA community had genuinely changed my career trajectory. If outreach programs could do that for me, maybe I could have a magnifying effect by helping shape not just my own path, but opening doors for others too.

So I joined CEA. I started helping run university group programs, scaled up to lead the groups team, and now oversee our community growth portfolio. Along the way, I created the Organizer Support Program (which now supports 100+ EA groups globally), helped incubate Kairos, and launched programs like EA Bootcamp and our pilot unis initiative.

How I work

I'm a bit obsessed with how work actually gets done. I track my time religiously, do Pomodoros with colleagues on Gather, and have strong opinions about Slack hygiene. I've kept a gratitude journal for 150+ days and read 15+ books on leadership and management this year alone.

I like structured work, well-prepared meetings, and keeping things moving. I give a lot of feedback and genuinely appreciate getting it back. Growth is kind of my thing—both for myself and for the people I work with.

This blog

I believe in learning in public. This blog is where I share productivity systems and workflows that actually work for me—time management, communication tools, how I structure my workday. Nothing fancy, just what's helped me get better at my job.

If something here resonates or you want to chat about any of it, feel free to reach out on LinkedIn or find me on the EA Forum.

Outside of work

When I'm not thinking about productivity systems, you can find me playing volleyball, doing yoga, or planning elaborate themed parties.
