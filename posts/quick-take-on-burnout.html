Quick Take on Burnout

February 7, 2025 · Wellbeing

Exhaustion vs. Burnout

I think it's important to distinguish between two states that often get conflated:

Exhaustion results from intense work hours and sleep deprivation. It's typically resolved with a short break or vacation (e.g., one week).

Burnout is more pervasive, affecting multiple life areas. It's deeper than exhaustion, and a short vacation isn't sufficient to resolve it.

What Actually Drives Burnout

Rather than pure overwork, burnout stems from more core feelings like motivation, recognition, and feeling good about the work you're doing.

The critical factor isn't hours worked—it's uncertainty about work value combined with lack of recognition.

Motivation is Key

I see motivation as moldable and critical to preventing burnout. One approach I advocate for: set designated times for re-evaluating the value of programs. This reduces constant worry while maintaining critical evaluation of impact.

If you're constantly questioning whether your work matters while doing the work, that's a recipe for burnout. Compartmentalizing reflection time helps.

Warning Sign

Getting sick more frequently than usual can indicate potential burnout risk. Illness can trigger a spiral: reduced productivity leads to increased stress, which leads to further illness.

If you notice you're getting sick often, it might be worth examining whether burnout is building up.

Work culture alone doesn't cause burnout—uncertainty about program value and neglecting motivation do.
