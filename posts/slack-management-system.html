My Slack Management System

June 26, 2025 · Productivity

Core principle: Treat Slack as asynchronous communication, even though it feels synchronous. If people need me immediately, they can call via Huddle or Facebook Messenger.

Step 1: Disable All Live Notifications

No pop-ups, no sounds, no bouncing icons at the bottom of the screen. This prevents constant interruption and context switching.

Step 2: Quick Triage Throughout the Day

I check Slack often throughout the day during Pomodoro breaks. For each message, I spend literally one second deciding: "Is this plausibly relevant for me?"

  • If not relevant (e.g., someone else's daily check-in that I'm not actively monitoring), mark as seen and move on
  • If potentially relevant, use "Remind me in 20 minutes" so it goes in saved for later

I have to be super disciplined here to not get sucked into Slack, and my pre-scheduled Pomodoros help make sure I don't spend too long.

The "Remind Me" Hack

The main goal is getting messages into Slack's remind-me list. To clear something from this list, you must actively check it off. This prevents accidentally losing important messages that got marked as read.

Step 3: Disciplined Inbox Clearing Sprints

I set a concrete goal: "I have 45 saved messages, getting below 10 by end of this Pomodoro." Goals make me work faster, and I often just beat them, which I consider evidence of them working.

I apply the Getting Things Done method:

  • If I can reply in under 2 minutes → do it now
  • If it takes less than 20 minutes → add to Sunsama as a subtask under "Smalls"
  • Organize Smalls into: immediate (today), later, backlog
  • If it takes more than 20 minutes → add to Sunsama as a task
  • If no action needed → check it off

Step 4: Slack-Free Focused Work

I never have Slack open during Pomodoros (except inbox clearing ones). If I need to reference Slack messages, I copy them into a Google Doc first. If I want to send a message mid-Pomodoro, I draft it in Google Doc and send after.

Most things can wait 25 minutes.

Step 5: Dedicated Small Task Sprints

I schedule focused time to knock out Slack-generated tasks under 15 minutes. Longer tasks get blocked into future days/weeks or deprioritized.

Key Insight

Separate "intake" (quick triage) from "processing" (quick responses, logging action points), and "doing" (taking action) to prevent constant context switching while ensuring nothing important gets lost.
